The callback issue was caused by the coordinator being busy when a worker finished. The callback was created as a queued task, but queue processing did not reliably resume, and the newer supervisor topology had no safe fallback owner that could continue the objective. The proposed repair is to have the exact coordinator session dispatch one worker, become idle, and reconcile the callback before choosing the next action. A later watchdog can nudge exceptional stalled work, but it should not write objective state or be treated as a guarantee. Historical completion counts show callback tasks often completed, but do not prove every workflow reconciled correctly or survived restarts.
