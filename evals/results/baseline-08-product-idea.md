The previous spawn/fork behavior could attribute a collaborator's child session to the parent owner, exposing the parent's application identity and credential context. The shipped safe default attributes cross-user spawns to the caller. A branch flag can opt back into parent-owner application attribution, but strict Unix mode still refuses execution when the caller-stamped Unix identity does not match that owner. The four longer-term designs are unscheduled sketches. The next useful decision is to collect evidence about real friction and demand from the flag before choosing a redesign direction; the current material does not justify selecting one.
